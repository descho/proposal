What is an Industry Research Project

The Industry Research Project (IRP) is the culmination of a Hyper Island Masters programme. We are expected to do research, come up with hypotheses, test them, and present a work-based thesis that adds value and challenges the industry. The output of the project could be a final report or a creative portfolio.

As part of their IRPs previous students have worked with large design firms like IDEO and AKQA, and social-good focused organisations like Impact Hub. I want to work on my IRP with you.

In Practice

There are two main research areas that I am interested in:

  1. Applying human-centred design and agile methodologies in the domain of public services and social innovation. This area is more process-focused.
  2. Inclusive design and accessibility. This has more to do with cognitive psychology. It includes questions like: How to design for everybody? How do skills, age and culture influence a person’s experience with a product or service?

These areas are broad on purpose for now. I plan to narrow my research question down based on the projects I will be working on and the needs of the organisation I partner with. If I work with FutureGov, I imagine spending 2/3 of the time doing UX/service design work on client and/or internal projects. And working on my research project the rest of the time. The output of my research may include blog posts, tool kits, or prototypes that I believe will be of value for FutureGov.

What I’ll need from you is regular feedback and a modest intern salary. I am open to discussing all details.
